I led development of a low-cost strip PET scanner using plastic scintillators and PMTs, with the goal of dramatically reducing the cost of PET-based cancer detection.

Highlights:

- Demonstrated a single-strip plastic scintillator PET prototype at the TAMU Physics Festival 2023.
- Achieved source localization within 1-inch error.
- Mentored undergraduates in scintillator preparation, DAQ setup, and Python analysis.
- Presented at multiple conferences and the TAMU Physics Festival 2024.
